In Graphite, each carbon atom is covalently bonded to three other carbon atoms

Thus, it has a giant covalent hexagonal structure,
and with weak intermolecular forces of attraction between oppositely charged ions,
causing it to be a good conductor of electricity, and a good choice as an anode material
