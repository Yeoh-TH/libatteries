In Graphite, each carbon atom is covalently bonded to three other carbon atoms

Thus, it has a giant covalent hexagonal structure in hexagonal layers,
one valence electron per carbon atom is delocalised within the structure;
these electrons are free to move to carry charges to conduct electricity
causing it to be a good conductor of electricity, and a good choice as
an anode material

As you can see...

In Silicon Carbide, one silicon atom and a carbon atom form 4 covalent bonds

Thus, it has a giant covalent tetrahedral structure, with each silicon and
carbon atom having strong intermolecular forces of attraction, thus
conducting electricity well, something that is needed in a battery.
However unlike graphite, it can be considered a semiconductor as
due to its energy levels in its valence shells, it does not conduct
electricty as well due to being unable to transfer charges as fast.
